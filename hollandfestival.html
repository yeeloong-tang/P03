Elevating Audience Engagement for Holland Festival with a Bespoke Visual Identity

Performing art enthusiasts may now look forward to a riveting digital touchpoint ahead of their highly anticipated day.

In collaboration with Sofia Cividin, Thu Le, Zhanyia Ye and Duc Ngo.

The "Exotica" is a digital touchpoint for Holland Festival that epitomises the physical sensations of itâ€™s performing art events. The microsite teases attendees with a captivating digital exploration that builds anticipation and engagement.

The festival lacked a touchpoint that effectively engaged users prior to the event, limiting their ability to explore and anticipate their festival experience. This gap presented an opportunity for a design intervention to enhance the audience's pre-engagement journey.

To ensure the robustness of the art direction, the team created various assets.

A modular approach that leads with a monochromatic color palette paired with intimately-lead-imagery strikes an intriguing atmosphere. Monospaced typography and a gridded approach distinguishes the spatial depth that serves as the basis for a concise and compelling visual language mirroring performance.

Usability considerations in the form of navigation bar, interactive cursor, progress bar, and language toggle ensures inclusion of global diverse users.

The introduction of interactive elements in the form of an interactive cursor prompts engagement that minimizes the identified gap, enabling users to easily explore with a compelling sense of wonder. Accessibility in the form of a navigation bar ensured that individuals who are goal oriented in their pursuit of information had a means to bypass the linear structure of the microsite.

The team employed a image interaction that empowers users with the freedom to enlarge images that pique their interests.

The proposed microsite successfully engages the gap in pre-engagement opportunities, providing a captivating yet informative digital experience that builds audience anticipation and engagement prior to the festival. Which illustrates the importance of identifying and addressing gaps in user engagement through thoughtful design interventions.
